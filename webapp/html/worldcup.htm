World cup 2015

The ICC World Cup 2015 will be held in Australia and New Zealand from February 14 to March 29, 2015. India are the defending champions as they won the last edition of the tournament held back in 2011 in the subcontinent.
A total of 14 teams will be taking part in this tournament. The teams have been divided in two pools — A and B — with seven teams filling up a group each.
Pool A consists of teams such as England, Australia, Sri Lanka, Bangladesh, New Zealand, Scotland and the newest entry being Afghanistan.
On the other hand, the Pool B consists of the defending champions India, the tournament's red-hot favourites South Africa, Pakistan, West Indies, Zimbabwe, Ireland and the United Arab Emirates (UAE).
A total of 49 matches will be played over the course of the tournament. Apart from the 10 Test-playing nations, Ireland and Afghanistan have managed to get through, which leaves two spots open for teams such as Netherlands, Kenya, Canada, and Scotland, among others.
Following the success of the ICC World Cup 2011 in the Indian sub-continent, the 2015 event is expected to be another action-packed clash of the top teams in world cricket. to 29 march.
